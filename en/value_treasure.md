# Value Treasure
*Find cheap giant companies the market has overlooked*

## 1. What is this Strategy?
- **Risk Level**: Low
- **Timeframe**: Long Term (1-12 months)
- **Target**: Value Investor, Long-Termer

Value Treasure finds large market-cap companies with low Price/Earnings (P/E) ratios. It's a digital version of Warren Buffett-style value investing. Discover giants with strong fundamentals ignored by the market.

## 2. Auto-Filter Parameters
When you click this card, the following filters are automatically applied:

| Column | Applied Value |
| :--- | :--- |
| Live Volume | > 1.000.000 |
| Market Cap | > 10B |

## 3. How It Works?
P/E ratio is filtered at a maximum of 12 and Market Cap is set to a minimum of 10,000M ($10 billion). Results are listed in ascending order of P/E.

## 4. Manual Tuning Guide
You can manually change the filters to fit your style:
- Lower the P/E upper limit to 8 to see ultra-cheap giants.
- Raise the Market Cap minimum to 50,000M to focus only on mega-cap companies.
- Set the sector filter to "Finance" to find value opportunities in banking.
- Set Change filter to -2 min and +2 max to see calm, stable giants.

## 5. Real-World Scenario
BRK.B (Berkshire Hathaway) has a P/E ratio of 9.5 and a market cap of $750B. While the market focuses on the tech rally, this giant is trading at a discount.

## 6. Risks & Warnings
- ⚠️ Low P/E doesn't always mean "cheap"—compare with the sector average.
- ⚠️ Some companies have low P/E for good reasons (stagnation, debt, etc.).
- ⚠️ Don't neglect balance sheet analysis to avoid "value traps."
