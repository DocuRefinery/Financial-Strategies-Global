# Cheap But Quality
*Big potential with a small budget—affordable quality stocks*

## 1. What is this Strategy?
- **Risk Level**: Medium
- **Timeframe**: Medium-Long Term (1-6 months)
- **Target**: Beginner Investor, Small Portfolio

Cheap But Quality finds stocks priced under $20 with a reasonable P/E ratio (under 25). You can buy many shares with a small budget while the company fundamentals remain decent.

## 2. Auto-Filter Parameters
When you click this card, the following filters are automatically applied:

| Column | Applied Value |
| :--- | :--- |
| Live Volume | > 1.000.000 |
| Market Cap | > 10B |

## 3. How It Works?
Price maximum $20, P/E ratio between 0.1-25, and Volume minimum 100,000. Results listed ascending by P/E.

## 4. Manual Tuning Guide
You can manually change the filters to fit your style:
- Lower price limit to $10 to find ultra-cheap but quality stocks.
- Lower P/E limit to 15 to be more selective.
- Add Market Cap minimum of 1,000M to reduce penny stock risk.
- Add Change minimum of 0 to see only cheap quality stocks rising today.

## 5. Real-World Scenario
Ford (F) trades at $12 with a P/E of 11. Big manufacturer, cheap price, reasonable valuation. Ideal entry point for small budgets.

## 6. Risks & Warnings
- ⚠️ Low price doesn't always mean "cheap"—always evaluate with P/E.
- ⚠️ Manipulation risk is high in stocks near the penny threshold (under $5).
- ⚠️ Check the company's debt/equity ratio.
