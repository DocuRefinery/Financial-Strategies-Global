# Volume Explosion
*Catch stocks where big players are quietly entering*

## 1. What is this Strategy?
- **Risk Level**: Medium
- **Timeframe**: Short Term (1-5 days)
- **Target**: Swing Trader, Day Trader

The Volume Explosion strategy identifies stocks seeing trading levels significantly above normal. Institutional investors, hedge funds, or insider buys often reveal themselves through volume spikes. This strategy lets you notice where "smart money" is flowing early.

## 2. Auto-Filter Parameters
When you click this card, the following filters are automatically applied:

| Column | Applied Value |
| :--- | :--- |
| Live Volume | > 1.000.000 |
| Market Cap | > 10B |

## 3. How It Works?
Filters the "Current Volume" column in the Screener table with a minimum value of 2,000,000 (2 million). Results are listed in descending order by volume, showing the most active stocks at the top.

## 4. Manual Tuning Guide
You can manually change the filters to fit your style:
- Raise the volume threshold to 5M to see only mega-volume stocks.
- Add a minimum of +1 to the Change (%) column to filter only rising volume explosions.
- Set the sector filter to "Technology Services" to track anomalies specifically in the tech sector.
- Set the P/E column to a maximum of 30 to eliminate overvalued speculative stocks.

## 5. Real-World Scenario
At market open, NVDA is trading at 3x its daily average volume. The Volume Explosion strategy brings this stock to the top. You then check the news—new GPU announcement. Early notice means early positioning.

## 6. Risks & Warnings
- ⚠️ High volume doesn't always mean a rise—volume also spikes during crashes.
- ⚠️ Fake volume pumps (pump & dump) are common in penny stocks.
- ⚠️ Always evaluate volume spikes alongside price changes.
